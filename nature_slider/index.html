AMAZON RAINFOREST
Known as the lungs of the Earth, the Amazon produces over 20% of the world's oxygen and supports incredible biodiversity.
PACIFIC OCEAN
The largest and deepest ocean on Earth, the Pacific regulates climate and is home to diverse marine life.
SAHARA DESERT
Covering most of North Africa, the Sahara is Earth's largest hot desert and plays a key role in climate and wind patterns.
ARCTIC TUNDRA
The frozen lands of the Arctic help regulate Earth’s temperature and are home to resilient wildlife like polar bears.
HIMALAYAN MOUNTAINS
The majestic Himalayas regulate the Asian climate, host sacred rivers, and shelter snow leopards and red pandas.
